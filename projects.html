Personal Project

Effects of Coronavirus on S&P Realized Volatility

After the Coronavirus pandemic spread to the United States, there was an understandable drop in the United States stock market. Also during this time it seemed like the stock market was more volatile than it was before the start of the pandemic. The purpose of this study was to quantify the effect Coronavirus had on the realized volatility of the S&P.

Final Project

Finding Nash Equilibrium in Zero Sum Sequential Games

Used a counterfactual regret minimization algorithm to develop an optimal strategy of playing Rock, Paper, Scissors and Kuhn's Poker. This was achieved by training the algorithm against itself over many iterations, revising its model in each round according to positive regret measurements. The image above shows the convergence to the Nash Equilibrum for the game Rock, Paper, Scissors. The full paper also describes finding the Nash Equilibirum for Kuhn's Poker, which is a zero sum sequential game.
